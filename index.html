Support Playlists – a short FAQ

I thought I would start our meeting with something simple. These are the questions I imagine you might have in your heads, and the most honest answers I can give.

FAQ 1
Are you here to pitch a product?
No. I do not have a product to pitch. I am not building anything and I am not trying to sell you anything. I am only here to talk about the human moment at the start of care.
FAQ 2
Are you expecting to join Rapid Health or take on a role?
No. I am not aiming for a job and I am not trying to become part of your structure. This is your world, not mine.
FAQ 3
Are you here to run a pilot or deliver anything operational?
Definitely not. I am not the right person to run delivery, operations or implementation. All of that sits with you.
FAQ 4
So what are you here for?
I am here because I keep seeing a gap between how people actually speak and how the system responds. People do not arrive with categories or symptoms. They arrive with stories. That gap keeps showing up, and Support Playlists comes directly out of it.
FAQ 5
What exactly is Support Playlists in one sentence?
Support Playlists turns what someone says in their own words into a short, curated sequence of next steps that actually fit their situation.
FAQ 6
Is this a module for Smart Triage?
It could be, but I do not know. That is entirely your domain. I am not imagining a specific feature. I am imagining the understanding layer, the human doorway, that sits upstream of whatever you choose to build.
FAQ 7
Are you trying to define your space inside Rapid Health?
No. I am trying to define my lane, not my place. My lane is emotional architecture, the human moment, the psychology of “I do not know where to start”. Everything beyond that is yours.
FAQ 8
What do you want from this meeting?
Three simple things:
1. Does the human moment I am describing feel true in your world.
2. Does this understanding layer feel useful alongside what you are already building.
3. If so, how might it fit in a way that keeps you fully in control.
FAQ 9
What if none of this fits?
Then that is completely fine. I am not attached to an outcome. I just want to offer a perspective that might be helpful.
FAQ 10
Why do this at all?
Because I think the system, and Rapid Health in particular, is missing one layer – the layer that listens to people as they actually are, before the system begins. If that layer is useful to you, I am happy to help clarify it. If not, that is okay too.
This page is just a thinking aid for our conversation. It is not a proposal or a product document.
Mark
